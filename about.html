◆ How it started
Origin story

Two players, one shared spreadsheet, and way too many browser tabs

Stackly began in 2023 as a shared spreadsheet between two friends who were tired of opening six different storefronts just to figure out what was worth downloading that weekend. The spreadsheet became a script. The script became a dashboard only the two of them used. Somewhere around the third friend asking to be added, it became obvious this needed to be a real product — not another wishlist tracker, but a live, honest answer to "what's actually trending right now."

Three years later, the spreadsheet is a pipeline processing tens of millions of events a day. The original rule hasn't moved: if a ranking can't be explained by real play data, it doesn't go on the page.

How we play

Four rules the team actually follows

Player-first, always

Every feature ships for the person deciding what to play tonight before it ships for a partner dashboard.

Signal over spin

If a ranking can't be traced back to real play data, it doesn't go on the page. No sponsored placements, no paid boosts.

Ship fast, stay honest

We push updates weekly, but we'll never backdate a number just to make a chart look better.

Built by players

Everyone on the data team still runs a personal account with alerts turned on. We use what we build.
